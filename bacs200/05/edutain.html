TED Talks

Captivating Software Design - Jackson Stone Get Your Brain to Focus - Chris Bailey The Psychopath Test - Jon Ronson
Jackson Stone recounts his experience in working with a boring interface, how this effected his workflow, and what he did about it.
  • Stone had an amazing software development opportunity to make data analysis fun and engaging
  • The problem was that because the program he was working with was incredibly stale, his workflow was interrupted and he found himself easily distracted
  • He was able to create a really cool and interactive design where users could visualize their projects
  • In his design, you can fly, which is pretty cool
 Author Chris Bailey demonstrates how our ability to focus is the key to productivity, creativity, and living a meaningful life, and helps us hone our own abilities to focus.
  • Disturbed by how much time he spends on his phone, Bailey sets out to "get rid of it" and limit his phone usage to 30 minutes a day for month, and what he found was a longer attention span, more ideas, and more plans for the future
  • It's not that our brains are ditracted, it's that our brains are overstimulated and crave those nuggets of distraction
  • Ideas came more often because his mind was not busy focused on something and had more opportunity to wander
  • "The state of our attention determines the state of our lives"
 With a captivating story, Jon Ronson illuminates just how blurry the line between the crazy and the sane actually is.
  • The story starts when Ronson meets a group of Scientologists who are determined to prove psychiatry is a pseudoscience, and they can prove it with a man named Tony
  • When Tony was 17, he beat someone up and was given the idea to fake madness to avoid a harsh prison cell, but he faked it too well and was sent to the Broadmoor Hospital (for the criminally insane)
  • His clinician stated that while they accept he faked madness, they believe he is a psycopath because faking madness is just the type of cunning and manipulative act of a psycopath
  • He ends the story with Tony, finally out of Broadmoore, telling him, "Everyone is a bit psychopathic"

Growth Mindset - Abi Stephenson The Paradox of Choice - Professor Renata Salecl The Internet in Society - Evgeny Morozov
Stephenson explains the idea of growth mindset and how impactful certain phrases can be on young minds.
  • Some students believe intellectual abilities are fixed while other believe it can develop, which tend to have impacts on their attitude
  • She explains three differences, or rules, between the two different attitudes in regards to learning
  • The way parents praise their children has a direct correlation to how the child will approach learning in the future
  • Intelligence praise turned kids off to learning, while process praise kept kids engaged and encouraged them to try new things even though they may be harder
 Professor Renata Salecl explores the anxiety and dissatisfaction surrounding limitless choice and questions if the amount of choice obstructs social change.
  • The choices we make invoke the need to be regarded by others
  • There is a constant need to make the ideal choice with the least loss and the most gain
  • Our ideology of choice forces us to place blame on ourselves for the failures in our lives
  • "The malaise in civilization and the malaise in the individual always go together"
  • The realization that we are in charge of our lives and not simply cogs in a machine is important for society to keep functioning
 Evgeny Morozov talks about how the internet may inhibit democracy and describes the notion of 'cyber-utopianism,' which is the idea that the internet plays a largely emancipatory role in global politics.
  • There is a famous quote which illustrates the naive idea that had blogs and the internet been around in the early 90's, the genocide in Rwanda never would have happened
  • The intended of use of technology is often confused with the actual use, especially in politics
  • In Thailand there is a website called Protect the King with the sole purpose of civilians submitting websites which might be offensive to the king (the offensive site then gets taken down within 24 hours)
  • To understand the true impact of technology on society you have to look much broader, and even moreso the negative consequences must be loked at as well
