The Effects of Early Technology Use in Children

Outline

Technology use has increased in younger generations

  • it is estimated that over 22% of adolescents log onto a social media website more than 10 times a day, and, with 75% of adolescents in general owning smartphones, this number is bound to increase
  • the next generation is largely growing up on their phones and tablets, which causes their social and emotional development to be reliant on this as well
  • young children have a limited sense of self-regulation and are especially impressionable which puts them at a higher risk of technologically induced psychological drawbacks
  • the use of technology at an early age negatively impacts the psychological development of young brains in three main areas

Focus

  • The starting point for all consequences, both positive and negative, is being able to even realize there is a problem
  • Being able to focus on a subject is the foundation set before a child can begin to learn and think for themselves
  • Wireless devices produce electromagnetic fields and pulsed radiofrequency radiation which have been documented to be especially detrimental to young people
  • Technology has shifted the way young brains develop because of this

Imagination

  • Focus also has a direct impact on imagination, a skill which is required to form opinions and questions about the world around them
  • Television and video games do the hard work of coming up with new ideas for them
  • These pre-set conditions, then, inhibit the growth of the problem-solving skills imagination fosters
  • This leaks into the way the child learns and may discourage them from learning altogether

Mental Illnesses

  • children have their own “child culture”
  • The constant use of social media, especially for children still figuring out who they are, can lead to a decrease in self-image and a much stronger need to feel accepted among their peers at an earlier age
  • The culture between children is then tainted with depression and possible cases of bullying
  • Children who are not yet capable of understanding the concept of depression should not already be feeling the effects of it

Conclusion

  • technology is not all bad and some professionals say it may enhance creative and communication skills and help children find their identity
  • but, because technology can be so entertaining, children tend to focus less on what is being said and more on the pictures surrounding the text
  • the habits of children are taught and are not pre-wired in their brains, which is why it is important to teach them healthy ones
